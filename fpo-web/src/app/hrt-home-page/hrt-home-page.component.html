PRIVACY NOTICE

The Tribunal collects personal information to process complaints filed under the Human Rights Code and to conduct surveys to evaluate and improve its services under s. 59.1 of the Administrative Tribunals Act.

The information statistical information on the previous page is confidential. The other personal information in this form will be disclosed to the other parties and to the Office of the Human Rights Commissioner and may be disclosed to the public as follows:

  • The Tribunal publishes most decisions on its website
  • The Tribunal publishes a hearing schedule (list of upcoming hearings) with the parties’ names and the areas and grounds of discrimination.
  • After a complaint is on the hearing schedule, the public has access to information, including the complaint and response forms (except contact information)
  • Hearings are open to the public.
  • The Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act applies to the Tribunal. Someone can apply to see information in the complaint file.

You can ask the Tribunal to limit the information it makes public. Use a form 7 to apply.

Other laws may restrict another party from going public with information in this complaint. You can also ask the Tribunal to order a publication ban. Use a Form 7 to apply.

The Tribunal will decide if your privacy interests outweigh the public interest in access to the Tribunal’s proceedings. For more information, see the Tribunal’s website, or contact the Tribunal Registrar at the address or phone number at the top of this form.
